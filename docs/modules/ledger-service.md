# Ledger Service

The ledger-service is responsible for recording and exposing financial postings.

---

## Responsibilities

- store immutable ledger entries
- persist postings generated by transaction-service
- validate ledger consistency constraints
- act as the source of truth for financial history
- support auditing and traceability
- enable balance reconstruction

---

## Ledger Model

Each business transaction generates one or more ledger entries.

Each ledger entry represents a **single account impact**.

Examples:

- a deposit generates one credit entry
- a withdrawal generates one debit entry
- a transfer generates two entries:
    - one debit entry
    - one credit entry

Entries are **append-only** and never updated.

---

## Immutability

Ledger entries are never modified or deleted.

This guarantees:

- auditability
- traceability
- consistency

Corrections must be represented by new entries, never by updating existing ones.

---

## Relationship with Transactions

The ledger-service does not create business transactions or define their structure.

It receives **pre-built ledger entries** from the transaction-service and persists them.

- `transactions` (managed by transaction-service) stores the business transaction context
- `ledger_entries` stores the financial postings generated from that transaction

Each ledger entry belongs to exactly one transaction.

---

## Double-Entry Consistency

The ledger enforces consistency rules over incoming entries:

- credits are stored as positive amounts
- debits are stored as negative amounts
- transfers must generate two ledger entries under the same `transaction_id`
- when multiple entries exist for a transaction, the sum of all `amount` values should be zero

---

## Relationship with Balance

The ledger does not store balances as a mutable field.

Balances are derived from:

- `ledger_entries` as the source of truth
- `daily_account_balances` as a materialized daily snapshot

---

## Data Ownership and Boundaries

The ledger-service owns its database schema and does not rely on foreign keys to external services.

- `account_id` is treated as an external reference
- account validation is performed by the transaction-service
- ledger-service focuses strictly on financial postings

This ensures service independence and aligns with microservice architecture principles.

---

## Persistence and Schema Management

- PostgreSQL is used as the database
- schema evolution is managed via Flyway migrations
- schema is versioned incrementally (`V1`, `V2`, ...)

The `ledger_entries` table is append-only and represents the core financial dataset.

---

## Query Capabilities

- list ledger entries by account
- filter entries by date range
- retrieve entries by transaction
- support audit queries

---

## Design Decisions

- append-only storage
- immutable ledger entries
- separation from transaction orchestration
- transaction-service is responsible for constructing ledger entries
- ledger-service is responsible for persisting and validating them
- independent from balance representation
- no foreign keys to external services (microservice boundary)
- database schema managed via migrations (Flyway)

---

## Future Improvements

- stronger consistency guarantees at persistence layer
- event streaming (Kafka or similar)
- projection-based balance calculation
- richer reconciliation and audit workflows
